Everyday Indian Cooking

Simple ways to incorporate millets into your daily meals

Popular Millet Recipes

Millet Rotis

Use millet flour to make soft, nutritious rotis. Mix with warm water, knead into dough, and roll out like regular rotis. Cook on a hot tawa until golden brown.

Tip: Add a pinch of salt and a teaspoon of oil for better texture.

Millet Dosas

Soak millet flour with urad dal, ferment overnight, and make crispy dosas. Perfect for breakfast with sambar and chutney.

Tip: Mix with rice flour for extra crispiness.

Millet Khichdi

Cook millet grains with moong dal, vegetables, and spices for a wholesome, easy-to-digest meal. Great for lunch or dinner.

Tip: Add ghee and cumin for enhanced flavor.

Millet Porridge

Cook millet flour with milk or water, add jaggery or honey, and top with nuts and fruits. A nutritious start to your day.

Tip: Use Ragi flour for extra calcium.

Millet Chilla

Make savory pancakes by mixing millet flour with water, adding vegetables, and cooking like regular chillas. High in protein and fiber.

Tip: Add grated vegetables for extra nutrition.

Millet Upma

Roast millet flour, add to tempered vegetables, and cook with water. A quick, healthy breakfast option.

Tip: Roast the flour well for better texture.

Tips for Switching from Wheat to Millet

1. Start Gradually

Begin with a 50/50 mix of millet and wheat flour. This helps your taste buds and digestive system adjust gradually.

2. Increase Millet Content

Once comfortable, gradually increase the millet proportion. Many people successfully transition to 100% millet within a few weeks.

3. Adjust Water Content

Millet flours may require slightly more or less water than wheat. Add water gradually while kneading to achieve the right consistency.

4. Experiment with Different Millets

Each millet has a unique flavor and texture. Try Ragi for rotis, Jowar for bhakris, and Bajra for winter comfort foods.

5. Store Properly

Keep millet flour in an airtight container, away from sunlight and moisture. This preserves freshness and prevents spoilage.

6. Enjoy the Nutty Flavor

Millets have a naturally nutty, earthy flavor that enhances the taste of your dishes. Embrace this unique characteristic!
